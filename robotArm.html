Exploritory Robotic Arm

The Project

I build this robot original several years ago and it was one of my first experiences with arduino programing. I use it as a testing platform for whatever new project I have at the time. Weather it be using potentiometers, running servos off a battery power, programing a record and playback feature or most recently learning to 3D print components parts. This overview is a summary of all the actions iv done with the robot arm in a more general sense. As i add on new projects i plan to update this page and document the newer process in more detail.

The Process

I began by assembling the metal pieces together with the motors. And mounted the arm onto a wooden board to keep it from falling over. Next I wired it to a power supply that delivers 12V and used a converter to drop it to 6V at 3 amps to power the motor. I used an arduino and a servo controller to map the servos movement to 6 potentiometers. Each potentiometer controls one of the six motors respectively. I fine turned each motor so that its max and min range would not extend past its physical limitations. For example the base motor cannot spin a full 360 without hitting either side of the metal frame. I also programmed a repeated pose button that would remember the motor locations of a given pose and return to it when pressed. Most recently I designed and printed parts for a new mounted system for the potentiometer to replace the breadboard they were previously on. The original design put too much weight on some of the potentiometers so I had to extend the first beam out to add a counter weight. Here you can see the most recent design. I will connect the wires to a solder breadboard and have it be a more interactive method of control for the Arm.

The Possible

I plan to keep adding to this project. In addition to finish wiring up the new potentiometer holders, which will hopefully be in the coming weeks. I want to add a vision system with a webcam so it can find and pick up objects. I also want to drive the robot with an app from my phone and inverse kinematics.
