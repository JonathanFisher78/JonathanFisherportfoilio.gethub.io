End of Summer

I wish I could hold you so tight,
To freeze time and still the world, an impossible plight.
I'd keep you safe from life's future pain,
The gift of summer, a moment to sustain,
To see you every day, sharing in your joy—
I'd never miss you or let fear deploy.

But if my arms never let you go,
The light you shine would lose its glow.
Your kindness and beauty swallowed by greed,
The Lord's plans for you lost and unseen.
You heal the hurting and bring others grace;
You train and you tame, and still get on base.
Yes, to possess you would bring me to tears,
More than the ones from your presence not near.

And so I embrace you, as this lesson I learn,
To cherish the love that I cannot earn.
I lock the moment in mind, not to pass,
And release with the hope that it won’t be the last.
I know our God has great things in store,
As we give Him our future, leaving this shore.
The comfort from Him conquers all strife—
How blessed we are to have been given this life.
